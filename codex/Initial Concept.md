# Theme

Goal of the game is to win the magical conflict between the mages figting for their influence 
over rapture peircing the reality in magical domains. 


# Core concepts

## Spell

   Spell is being build by each player from tiles sourced from *tile offer*. Each tile added
   to the spell represents one magical word and most of them holds certain effect that is
   either passive and takes effect by adding the word to the spell or active and its effect
   taks place only when the spell is released. 
   Each turn player can decide to expand their spells or release them.

## Status deck
   Status deck servers as a representation of mage`s mental and physical state. Spells adds 
   status cards to the deck and every turn there is certain ammount of such effects active 
   for each mage. Theese status cards are re-drawn each time *Reallignment* is reached.
   Drawing too many negative effects during the *Reallignment* can lead to death 
   and player elimination. In addition If at any time during the *Reallignment* 
   player should draw more cards from their deck then there are cards in the deck 
   the player becomes exhausted and is eliminated.
   
## Domains 
   Domains are unpredictible dimensions of pure magical force. Several of these domains leaks
   to their power to the real world through the rapture players are trying to sieze 
   control off. Each domain provides its own stability track that is filled with *Power tokens*
   at the start of the game. Each *spell* requires at the very least one *power token*
   to function, but draining the power from the domain progresses it towards the *instability*.
   In addition to all this, releasing a spell marks player with the token of used domain which
   can lead to grave consequences once the domain reaches its point of instability. 
   
## Tile offer
   Tile offer represent the options players have to use the powers of the domains leaking 
   through the rapture. Limited number of tiles are available at any point during the game 
   and when the offer gets closer to being depleted, the realignment phase is triggered.
   
# Game structure
    
   Game is played in two altering phases. 
        - Weawing phase 
        - Reallignment phase

## Weawing phase
Durign this phase players takes their turn in clockwise manner. Every turn there axactly two
options for the player by default. 
   - Weawe the spell 
   - Release the spell
If there is no valid way to add additional tile to the spell, player is forced to release the 
spell. 
In addition to these two options, player can activate any of their current status cards placed
in front of them. Activation of the status cards is not limited and can be done before or after 
the main action choice. The rules for the activation are explained on the card itself.
 
 ### Weawing the spell
 Take a tile from the *tile offer* and add it to your current spell. While doing so, you have
 to follow the tile requirments. (If tile requires certain bumber of PT types, 
 you have to have such PTs acrocc all of the cores of your spell.)
 When you add the tile to the spell, imidietly apply its effect. You can choose from single 
 use effects provided by domains or the allways available effects. Alternatively, you can 
 choose an status card of appropriete type and add this card to either your discard pile or 
 to the spell pile. 
 If you choose to add status card to your own spell, take a token of corresponding type and 
 add place it near the spell deck as a remainder. This tokens will be usefull once 
 the spell is released.
 Instead of addin a tile from the offer to your spell, you can choose to add new core to your
 spell. If you choose so, take any tile from the offer and return it to the bag. Then choose 
 any of your available cores, add it to the spell and then fill all emty slots with any 
 combination of PTs from the domain boards.
 #### Ripple
    Weawing the spell by using tiles with ripple token on them will cause the *Ripple* 
    in the magical substrate. Theese ripples are dangerous for other spells. If such a tile 
    is used for weawing check the Rune and Alignment of the tile used. All other tiles in 
    already existing spells are imidetly destroyed. If the destroyed tile creates a situation
    when some part of the spell becomes disjointed from the spell core, destroy those 
    dijointed tiles as well as the tile targeted by the ripple. 
    Remove the cards from the spell deck and status deck accordingly. 
 ### Releasing the spell 
   When you decide to, or when you are unable to weawe the spell anymore, you have to release 
   the spell. To release the spell do following:
   1. Choose any player as a target
   2. Take tokens placed near your spell pile and your status deck. Turn the cards 
     one by one and every time you turn a card that corresponds with a type specified on any 
     of the tokens, return that card and that token back to the offer. 
   3. Shuffle the spell pile, if there is any, to the status deck pile of target player. 
   4. Return all the spell tiles to the bag. 
   5. Place one of the PTs used in the spell in front of you to mark yourself with that domain.
   6. Return remaining PTs to the corresponding domain boards. 
  
 
## Reallignment phase
Reallignment represent unpredictibale nature of the rapture itself. The conditions constantly
change and the fabric of reality wraps and morphs. After each reallignment new options emerge,
others are lost and statuses of players are refreshed. 
Relignment is triggered if any player should take their turn but the *Tile offer* 
contains less tiles then there is players currently in the game. 
During this phase following happens in this order: 
    - Power leak (removing PT from the most stable domain.)
    - Instability check
        - Instability resolution
    - Status redraw (taking the potentital instabilities into account...)
    - Health/Exhaustion check
    - Offer refill
    - Pass first player token to the player who played last in weing phase
    
 


## Spell tiles
  ### Word tiles
  Word tiles are sourced from random, periodically refreshing offer and acts as a sources 
  or spell effects that are gathered in either spell deck or directly in status deck
  of the spell owner.

  Word tiles contains following information:
  1. 1-3 Rune(s) - symbol required by spell effect cards
  2. 1 Alignment symbol - symbol used by *Ripple* and other effects
  3. 1-5 Connector points - sides of the spell where other tiles can be added to
