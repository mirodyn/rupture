Game modes and winning the game

The Goal of the game depend on the selected game mode. In "Free for All" mode, the goal is to be the last mage still standing. The game ends whne there is only one player left and thats the winner. In "Team" mode, the goal is to overome the opposing team. When there is only one team left, the game ends and the remaining team is victourious.

Game structure

Game is split into two altering phases:
  • Action phase
  • Realignment phase
During the Action phase players takes turns in building and releasing their spells.
During the Reallignment phase reality adjusts, new options are presented and already casted spells can take their tolls. During these two phases events can occure. These events break the standard flow of the game and every time event is triggered, its fully resolved before the normal flow of the game resumes. Events can even trigger other events. If this happens, the most recently triggered event has to be resolved before the resolutions of the causing event is resumed.

Action phase

Action phase is divided into rounds. During each round, players take turns starting with the player holding the Initiative token. During the turn, player MUST take precisely ONE of the following three actions:

Anchor

Anchoring the spell to a domain opens up many possibilities ...
To anchor the spell to any domain do the following:

1. Select one Rune from any Well and return it to the bag.
Can trigger Ripple event (page ).
2. Take a Shard from any of the Domain tracks.
Can trigger Instability event (page ).
3. Attach Core to your spell and place the Shard on it. If you dont have a Spell yet, just place the Core in your area. That's your new Spell!

You can have at most 4 Core tiles as a part of your spell!

Weave

Thread by thread, that's how the magic is formed ...
To weave the spell to any domain do the following:

Take one Rune from any Well.
Can trigger Ripple event (page ).
Attach the Rune to your Spell
(Optional) acquire 1 Incantation card from the offer.
See (page ).

You can only attach Runes to other Runes or Cores in your Spell. Runes can't ever be disconnected from the rest of the Spell.
See Collapse (page ).

Release

Who knows what can happen when magic runs wild ...
To release the spell to any domain do the following:

Put all cards from your Spell deck on top of ANY Player deck.
Discard all Incantation cards with Focus token placed on top of them from your Active area.
Return all Runes from your spell to the bag and all the Cores to the supply.
Keep all the Shards that were in your Spell in your Player area and acquire Resonance card(page ) for each one of them.

In addition to these three actions, Resonance cards can provide players with additional actions to take.
Action phase ends when there are no more Runes available across all the Wells.

Acquiring Incantation cards

There are 12 Incantation cards available across 4 Domain offers. Players can choose to acquire one of them at the end of the Weave action IF they fulfill the requirments specified by the card itself. The requirments are specified by the Formula printed on the left side of the card.

To check if the Formula is fulfilled by the spell do the following:

1. Find in your Spell ALL uninterupted paths from Core with Shard of corresponding Domain symbol to the last connected Rune (Key Rune).
There can be multiple existing paths. Path is broken by opened tile connectors or Core. No tile can occure more then once on the same path.
2. Look at the paths you found in the previous step. IF there is at least one path ending with Rune sequence, the Formula is fulfilled and the Incantation can be taken.
3. If is the Formula fulfilled you can take the Incantation and put it face-down on top of your Spell deck or face-up in your Active area with Focus token on it.
Effects of Incantations added to your Active area become active, once the current turn is over.
